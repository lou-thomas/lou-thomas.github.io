How your culture has influenced your values and identity?

My culture has influenced my value of independence and variety- it's so common for Kiwis to want to go out and explore the world. I think we sit here at the 'bottom' of the world and for me I didn't get the opportunity to go anywhere else untill I was an adult so by then I was bursting with curiosity about other places and lives. The great OE is such a common concept here but not so in other cultures.

I value personal freedom combined with trust and respect. I've been fortunate enough as a Pakeha to grow up under systems and governments that overall I trust. Plus a culture that doesn't require 100% conformity. I love the outdoors and I have a sense of resilience - as mum puts it - quiet determination - I think that is a little bit the Kiwi psyche, we are quiet about our achievements but we do strive to find our place in the world.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

There has occassionaly been tension at work when someone has displayed immaturity and managed to offend others and myself, despite having a heart in the right place. Initial strategy was to recognise that any sense of disharmony can really affect my mood and sense of calm/focus. So I removed myself and didn't get involved. Eventually I reached out to a trusted person and they could validate I wasn't the only one feeling that way. They were brave enough to try to help the person understant what had happened. I'd like to get to a place where I am both confident and skilful enough to do the same. Particularly when I feel the person is willing to change/grow/be the best they can be.
