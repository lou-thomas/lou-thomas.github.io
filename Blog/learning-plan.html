Learning Plan

Long term goals or career path

I'm honestly not entirely sure where I want this to take me. I do know that so far I'm really enjoying learning and I think I'd like to start out as a junior developer and see how it feels to be paid to do this! So very short term I want to succeed at EDA. From there I want to put the skills to use in a work context. After some experience hopefully I will still enjoy it and be confident enough to be in a position where I am mixing my coding/technical skills with my social values. I think that the satisfaction of developing will only last in the long run, if I believe in the end result. Essentially the Why.

Dare I put in writing that actually I want it all. The fulfilling career where I am challenged and feel like I'm making a difference and being paid nicely for it. Plus the flexibility or worklife balance to live in Greece and spend afternoons eating grapes in the sun.

Things that will hold me up or make me better at learning

First up I'm concerned about my habit of procrastination. I have found it useful so far to timebox but I need to get stricter. I will continue to look at the current time when I start a challenge and work out when I should be finished or when I am going to stop for a break. Write it down and stick to it. Use alarms.

It's almost been a good thing that I am working part time during Foundations as not having a full day for study forces me to be focussed during the time I do have. I will aim to take this as a positive lesson. During bootcamp I will try to keep a reasonable amount of time for the important things such as personal commitments and exercise. Rather than giving myself a whole evening to sttudy for example, actually setting the time aside to go out for a walk with a friend will benefit me for the fresh air but also as long as it's planned, I can factor that in to the available hours I have that day and use it to keep me focussed (read scared of getting behind).

I suspect my age and general life experience will help me deal with the stress, with knowing when to speak up and putting things in perspective. Mind you it's been a while since I really put myself out there and took on something as new and challenging as this so I hope I really do ask for help when I think I will. I naturally enjoy supporting others so hopefully that becomes reciprocal and I can accept the help when needed.

How I'll work with peers and facilitators and the EDA community

From a facilitator I ask for honest feedback and if need be, a fairly tough or direct approach if I'm falling behind.I will do what works best for me which is taking some time to consider feedback before acting or responding to it.

I really value community and believe a lot can be achieved when people have a sense of belonging so I will do my best to nurture that side of the experience as well. This looks like participating in slack conversations, being prepared to be a little vulnerable if I am stuck and always acting with respect for each other

Non tech skills

It has been really energising learning stuff these last 2 weeks and I think part of what I want to take away is firstly the passion for learning, secondly the skills for focussed learning, taking good notes instead of only being in the moment learning. I think to be a good developer you do need to keep up to date and be willing to try new things. I hope I can hone skills of extra curricular learning to do this instead of just sticking to what's needed on the day.
